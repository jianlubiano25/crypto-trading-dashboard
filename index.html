Break of Structure, Change of Character, and trend analysis fundamentals

Break of Structure (BoS)
Occurs when price closes above/below a significant high/low, confirming trend continuation or reversal.
Change of Character (ChoCh)
Signals a shift in market structure from bullish to bearish or vice versa. Often marks the start of a new trend.

‚ö° Fair Value Gaps

Fair Value Gap (FVG)
A price imbalance or inefficiency between candles, often revisited by price. Used for entries or exits.

üéØ EQH/EQL

Equal Highs (EQH)
Indicates potential liquidity above resistance. Price may hunt these levels before reversing.
Equal Lows (EQL)
Suggests liquidity below support. Often targeted in stop hunts before price moves up.
Strong High
A swing high with strong rejection and no follow-through. Often unbroken for long periods.
Weak Low
A swing low with multiple tests and little bounce. Likely to be broken.

üü¢ Bullish Patterns

Bullish Engulfing
Two-candle reversal at the bottom of a downtrend. A large green candle fully engulfs the prior red candle. Shows buyer strength. Confirmation with a break above the green candle is ideal.
Hammer
Reversal pattern after a downtrend. A small body on top with a long lower wick. Indicates strong buying after a price drop. Best confirmed with a green candle closing above the high.
Morning Star
Three-candle bullish reversal after a downtrend. Pattern: Long red candle ‚Üí small indecision ‚Üí strong green candle closing into first candle's body. Indicates sellers lost control. Confirmation is when green candle closes above midpoint of red candle.
Inverted Hammer
Bullish reversal pattern with a small body at the bottom and a long upper wick. Similar to a hammer but inverted. Suggests potential reversal from downtrend. Confirmation needed with follow-up bullish candle.
Three White Soldiers
Three consecutive long green candles with small wicks. Each candle opens within the previous candle's body and closes higher. Strong bullish momentum indicator. Best in oversold conditions.

üî¥ Bearish Patterns

Bearish Engulfing
Two-candle reversal at the top of an uptrend. A large red candle fully engulfs the prior green candle. Signals sellers overpowering buyers. Confirmation is a close below the red candle's low.
Shooting Star
Bearish reversal after an uptrend. A small body near the bottom with a long upper wick. Suggests buyers were rejected at highs. Confirmation is a close below the star's low.
Evening Star
Three-candle bearish reversal at the top of an uptrend. Pattern: Long green candle ‚Üí small indecision ‚Üí long red candle into first candle's body. Indicates buyers losing strength. Confirmation is a close below the midpoint of the first candle.
Hanging Man
Bearish reversal pattern that looks like a hammer but appears at the top of an uptrend. Small body at the top with long lower wick. Suggests potential reversal. Confirmation needed with bearish follow-up.
Three Black Crows
Three consecutive long red candles with small wicks. Each candle opens near the previous candle's close and closes lower. Strong bearish momentum indicator. Best in overbought conditions.

üü£ Neutral/Indecision Patterns

Doji
A Doji has equal open and close prices, forming a cross shape. It signals market indecision and possible trend exhaustion. Appears after long bullish or bearish candles. Confirmation with the next candle is advised.
Spinning Top
Small body with long upper and lower wicks. Indicates market indecision and potential trend reversal. The longer the wicks, the more indecision. Confirmation needed from subsequent candles.

üìà Trend Direction Indicators

Moving Averages (MA)
Simple Moving Average (SMA): Average price over a specific period. Most common: 20, 50, 100, 200 periods.

Exponential Moving Average (EMA): Gives more weight to recent prices. Faster response to price changes.

Usage: Price above MA = bullish trend, below = bearish trend. Golden Cross (50 MA crosses above 200 MA) = bullish signal.
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
Components: MACD line (12 EMA - 26 EMA), Signal line (9 EMA of MACD), Histogram (MACD - Signal).

Bullish Signals: MACD crosses above signal line, histogram turns positive, bullish divergence.

Bearish Signals: MACD crosses below signal line, histogram turns negative, bearish divergence.

Best Used: Trend confirmation and momentum shifts.
Parabolic SAR
Purpose: Trend following and stop-loss placement.

Bullish: Dots below price indicate uptrend.

Bearish: Dots above price indicate downtrend.

Exit Signal: When dots flip to opposite side of price.

Settings: Default 0.02 acceleration, 0.2 maximum.
ADX (Average Directional Index)
Purpose: Measures trend strength, not direction.

Components: ADX line, +DI (positive directional indicator), -DI (negative directional indicator).

Strong Trend: ADX > 25 indicates strong trend.

Weak Trend: ADX < 20 indicates ranging market.

Signal: +DI above -DI = bullish, -DI above +DI = bearish.
Ichimoku Cloud
Components: Tenkan-sen (9-period average), Kijun-sen (26-period average), Senkou Span A & B (cloud), Chikou Span (lagging).

Bullish: Price above cloud, Tenkan above Kijun, cloud green.

Bearish: Price below cloud, Tenkan below Kijun, cloud red.

Support/Resistance: Cloud acts as dynamic support/resistance.

Best Used: Multiple timeframe analysis and trend confirmation.

‚ö° Momentum & Oscillator Indicators

RSI (Relative Strength Index)
Purpose: Measures speed and magnitude of price changes.

Overbought: RSI > 70 indicates potential reversal down.

Oversold: RSI < 30 indicates potential reversal up.

Divergence: Price makes new high/low but RSI doesn't = reversal signal.

Settings: Default 14 periods, can use 9 for faster signals.
Stochastic Oscillator
Components: %K line (current price vs range), %D line (3-period SMA of %K).

Overbought: Above 80, potential reversal down.

Oversold: Below 20, potential reversal up.

Bullish Signal: %K crosses above %D in oversold area.

Bearish Signal: %K crosses below %D in overbought area.

Settings: Default 14,3,3 (periods, %K smoothing, %D smoothing).
Williams %R
Purpose: Momentum oscillator measuring overbought/oversold levels.

Range: 0 to -100 (inverted scale).

Overbought: Above -20, potential reversal down.

Oversold: Below -80, potential reversal up.

Signal: Exit overbought/oversold zones.

Settings: Default 14 periods.
CCI (Commodity Channel Index)
Purpose: Identifies cyclical trends and overbought/oversold conditions.

Range: Typically ¬±100, but can extend beyond.

Overbought: Above +100, potential reversal down.

Oversold: Below -100, potential reversal up.

Zero Line: Crossing above/below zero indicates trend change.

Settings: Default 20 periods.
MFI (Money Flow Index)
Purpose: Volume-weighted RSI, combines price and volume.

Range: 0 to 100.

Overbought: Above 80, potential reversal down.

Oversold: Below 20, potential reversal up.

Divergence: Price/volume divergence signals reversal.

Settings: Default 14 periods.

üìä Volatility & Range Indicators

Bollinger Bands
Components: Middle band (20 SMA), Upper/Lower bands (¬±2 standard deviations).

Squeeze: Bands contract = low volatility, expansion coming.

Expansion: Bands widen = high volatility, trend continuation.

Support/Resistance: Price often bounces off bands.

Breakout: Price breaking bands = strong momentum.

Settings: Default 20 periods, 2 standard deviations.
ATR (Average True Range)
Purpose: Measures market volatility, not direction.

High ATR: High volatility, larger price movements expected.

Low ATR: Low volatility, smaller price movements expected.

Stop Loss: Use ATR multiplier for dynamic stop placement.

Position Sizing: Higher ATR = smaller position size.

Settings: Default 14 periods.
Keltner Channel
Components: Middle line (20 EMA), Upper/Lower bands (ATR multiplier).

Trend Following: Price staying within channels indicates trend.

Breakout: Price breaking channels = potential trend change.

Mean Reversion: Price returning to middle line.

Settings: Default 20 EMA, 2 ATR multiplier.
Donchian Channel
Components: Upper (highest high), Lower (lowest low), Middle (average).

Breakout Trading: Price breaking upper/lower channels.

Range Trading: Price bouncing between channels.

Trend Strength: Channel width indicates volatility.

Settings: Default 20 periods.

üìà Volume & Market Structure

Volume Analysis
High Volume: Confirms price movement, shows strong conviction.

Low Volume: Weak conviction, potential reversal.

Volume Divergence: Price up but volume down = weak move.

Volume Climax: Extremely high volume often marks tops/bottoms.

On-Balance Volume (OBV): Cumulative volume indicator for trend confirmation.
VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price)
Purpose: Average price weighted by volume, fair value indicator.

Bullish: Price above VWAP, especially with volume.

Bearish: Price below VWAP, especially with volume.

Support/Resistance: VWAP often acts as dynamic level.

Best Used: Intraday trading and institutional analysis.
Accumulation/Distribution Line
Purpose: Measures buying/selling pressure based on price and volume.

Accumulation: Line rising = smart money buying.

Distribution: Line falling = smart money selling.

Divergence: Price vs A/D line divergence signals reversal.

Confirmation: A/D line confirming price trend.
Chaikin Money Flow
Purpose: Volume-weighted average of accumulation/distribution.

Range: -1 to +1.

Bullish: Above +0.25, strong buying pressure.

Bearish: Below -0.25, strong selling pressure.

Neutral: Between -0.25 and +0.25.

Settings: Default 20 periods.
Volume Profile
Purpose: Shows volume at different price levels.

Value Area: 70% of volume occurs here, key support/resistance.

Point of Control: Price level with highest volume.

High Volume Nodes: Strong support/resistance areas.

Low Volume Nodes: Weak areas, price moves through quickly.

Best Used: Swing trading and position sizing.
